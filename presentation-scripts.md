# Presentation Scripts — SunVault Capstone

Two videos. The **Loom** is the polished client presentation (the hero deliverable). The
**Raven** is your behind-the-scenes Day-5 update for your instructor. Stage directions in
[brackets]; say the rest.

---

## 🎥 LOOM — Client Presentation (~4–6 min)
Present AS SunVault's agency, TO the client. Screen-share throughout.

**[Open on the live landing page — https://sun-vault-ashen.vercel.app/]**
> "Hi — I want to walk you through the complete lead-generation system we built for **SunVault**,
> your luxury smart-solar brand. The whole idea is one promise: *see exactly what you save, down
> to the watt, from day one.* Most installers put panels up and disappear — SunVault gives
> homeowners a system they can actually watch work, for life. Here's how we turn a stranger into
> a booked call."

**[Scroll the landing page — hero/live monitor, the difference, savings, how-it-works, testimonials, FAQ]**
> "This is the storefront. The live energy monitor in the hero *is* the product. Dual currency,
> dollars and pesos, for your market. Real numbers, and one clear action everywhere: book a Solar
> Savings and Design Call."

**[Click a 'Book a call' button → GHL calendar opens]**
> "That button goes straight to a real booking calendar. When someone picks a time..."

**[Switch to GoHighLevel — Opportunities board]**
> "...they land right here on the pipeline — the heart of the system. New Lead, Consult Booked,
> Site Assessment, Proposal Sent, Won. 17 leads, about $110,000 in pipeline value, real sources:
> Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, referrals."

**[Show the Instagram carousels + Facebook ad draft]**
> "This is the creative that feeds the top of the funnel — an Instagram carousel and a Facebook
> ad, on-brand, pointing back to that page."

**[Open a branded email template in GHL]**
> "Under the hood, the emails — designed and branded, not plain text. A booking confirmation and
> a pre-call nurture that warms the lead up before the call."

**[Open Automation → Workflows, show A1/A2/A3]**
> "And this is the engine. Three automations: one connects a booking to the pipeline and sends the
> confirmation, one nurtures before the call, and one chases any proposal that goes quiet for three
> days. Nobody clicks send. The system does."

**[Live test booking → contact + opportunity appear + email fires]**
> "Let me show you live. I'll book a slot now... and there — a new contact, a new card in Consult
> Booked, and the confirmation email fires automatically."

**[Back to landing page]**
> "So that's the whole system: ad, to page, to booking, to pipeline, to automated follow-up — all
> on-brand, all connected, running on its own. That's SunVault: luxury solar that thinks. Thanks
> for watching."

## 🎥 RAVEN — Instructor Day-5 Update (~2–4 min)
Your build update, not a client pitch.

> "Hey — here's my Day 5 capstone. I built a full lead-gen system for an invented business,
> **SunVault**, a luxury solar installer whose edge is IoT real-time monitoring."

**[Show the file structure / GitHub repo]**
> "I built it in layers: the pipeline in GoHighLevel — 17 leads across 6 stages with custom fields
> and a booking calendar. Then the landing page — built from scratch in HTML for full design
> freedom, deployed on Vercel. Then the carousel and Facebook ad, the branded emails, and three
> automations that connect everything."

**[Show the landing page hero]**
> "What I'm most proud of is the landing page and its live energy-monitor hero. I used AI image
> generation for a cinematic hero background and two Instagram carousel sets, then composited my
> own logo for brand consistency."

**[Show the opportunity import / a tricky moment]**
> "The trickiest part was importing opportunities into GoHighLevel — it needs actual Contact IDs,
> not just emails, so I exported the contacts, merged the IDs back in, and re-imported."

> "With more time I'd add a hot-lead router for high-bill leads and SMS alongside the emails. But
> the core system is live and every piece connects end to end. That's SunVault."

---

## Recording tips
- **Loom:** camera bubble on, screen-share, do the live test booking for the wow moment.
- **Raven:** more casual, show the repo and the build, be honest about what was hard.
- Keep both tight — rehearse once, record in one take, don't over-edit.
- Have the landing page, GHL board, and a workflow open in tabs before you start.
